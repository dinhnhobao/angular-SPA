Frequently Asked Questions

I am interested. How do I start?

Please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve indicating your NUSNET ID. We will register you for a SDP account so you can develop and deploy your product there.

I am currently hosting my project on this platform. How do I port my stuff over to SDP?

Please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve indicating your NUSNET ID. Please indicate as much details about your project as possible so that we can help you migrate your product to SDP.

I need customisations or other requests for my product to deploy to SDP.

Please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve indicating your NUSNET ID. Please indicate as much details about your project as possible so that we can assist you.

I am a non-Computing students. Can I join?

Yes sure! Students from all NUS faculties are welcomed to use this platform.

How do I create an account for SDP?

Please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve indicating your NUSNET ID. We will register you for a SDP account so you can develop and deploy your product there.

Do I own the code for the projects deployed to the Student Development Platform?

Yes. You own the code. However, NUS has the right to publish the applications campus-wide.

What are the rights to my application?

Students own the code. However, NUS has the right to publish the applications campus-wide.

Do I need to comply with any requirements or restrictions? Is there any kind of content that will not be allowed?

The applications should be relevant to the NUS community. Additionally, your applications must not include any of the following content: Infringing Content, Nudity or Sexual Content, Illegal Content, Harmful or Dangerous Content, Hateful Content, etc.

My product is not that relevant to the NUS community. Can I still join SDP?

Please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve indicating your NUSNET ID. We will assess your project to see if it fulfills the criteria.

Do I need to make sure that my code doesn't have security vulnerabilities?

Yes. Your code will be scanned for vulnerabilities and we will make the necessary changes to ensure the security of your project as well as the whole platform.

Will I be given administrative or root access for my self-service EC2 instance?

Yes. You will be given root privilege to install the necessary tools for your product to aid you in the development and deployment process.

Do I need to pay for hosting/storage on SDP?

No. ALL of the cost is on us.

What technologies are currently available?

Amazon EC2 (Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud) is currently available at the moment. There will be more features and services provided by SDP in the next few months. Stay tune.

How much storage is available for my product?

You will be given as much storage as needed to scale your project, as long as the storage is relevant to the application. You can freely develop your product on the platform, storage and hosting fees are on us!

Non-NUS students contributed to my project. Can I deploy my project on SDP? Do non-NUS students have access to the platform?

Your project should have substantial contribution from NUS students. If you are unsure, please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve for further assistance. Your project will possibly be able to deploy on SDP. However, non-NUS students won't be given access to our SDP. NUS students can share their accounts with non-NUS contributors for them to access the platform.

What licenses are we gonna use for the platform?

SDP uses (a variation of) MIT License. If your open-source project hasn't adopted a license, we shall use SDP's license. If your project is currently using MIT License, great! There should be not much of licensing issues when integrating to SDP. If your project is using stricter licenses e.g. Apache or GNU, please email Mr. Steve at Mr Kwa Lam Koon, Steve for further assistance.

How secure is your platform?

Our platform is very secure. Every project on board will be scanned for vulnerabilities and we will make the necessary changes to ensure the security of each product and the platform in general.
